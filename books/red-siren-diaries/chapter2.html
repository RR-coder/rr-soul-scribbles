Chapter 2: The Idol Vanishes

A call shattered the uneasy calm of a Tuesday morning at Hauz Khas Police Station. ACP Jai Deep Rana, eyes buried in a half-read file and mind flickering back to his last case, was jerked out of thought by Inspector Aditya Mishra’s rushed entry.

"Sir, museum mein chori ho gayi hai. Hauz Khas ke heritage wing se ek bahut hi rare murti gayab hai!"

"Which idol?" Jai Deep asked, alert now.

"Sir… ek Vishnu murti thi, bronze ki, 9th century. Aur… yeh sunke aapka dil aur toot jayega—wo murti Muzaffarpur ke ek purane mandir se thi, jo museum ko donate ki gayi thi."

Something stirred inside Jai Deep. Muzaffarpur… Baba ke saath wahi mandir gaya tha main…

Scene: Hauz Khas Museum

No sign of forced entry. CCTV footage—conveniently corrupted. Security guard missing. A flawless job.

"Sir, yeh toh jadoo hai. Na darwaza toda, na tala. Jaise murti khud chal ke chali gayi ho," Mishra ji quipped, chewing on a samosa.

"Kha ke aa gaye ho thane ya udhar hi thele par post lag gayi thi tumhari?"

"Sir thoda bhukh lagal rahe, operation se pehle peet bhara rahe."

"E Mishra, agar ek baar aur tum samosa ke naam pe investigation ka time waste kiya na… Bihar bhej dunga traffic control mein!"

"Arre sir, aap toh gussa ho gaye!"

Press Conference

Media frenzy outside the police station.

"ACP Rana, are you saying there’s a theft racket going on in Delhi’s heritage system?"

"No comment until investigation concludes," Jai Deep replied, jaw tight.

Later, DCP called him: "Don't blow this up, Rana. Just recover the idol. Quietly."

But Jai Deep wasn’t built for quiet cover-ups.

Investigation Leads

Black-market smuggler Bhurelal Kashyap was nabbed in Daryaganj. A pen drive was recovered—encrypted. Jai Deep sent it to cyber specialist Sana Zaidi.

"It’s a miracle this idiot even encrypted it. File name: smuggling_plan_finalfinal2.docx. Genius."

Inside: a list of idols, buyers, and destinations. One marked: VK Shipping – Colombo. Another name: Raabta.

Flashback: Jai Deep (Age 10)

"Papa, yeh murti kaun hai?"

"Vishnu. Rakhwala. Tumhara duty bhi yahi hoga ek din. Raksha karna."

He never wanted me in police. But maybe I became this for him… even if he won’t admit it.

Undercover Sting – Okhla

Mishra ji wore a fake beard. Jai Deep wore a fake temper.

They posed as buyers from Singapore.

"Tum Singapore se ho?"

"Haan bhaiya, hum Singapore ke Sitamarhi side se hain."

Jai Deep almost coughed.

"Idol laayo."

One bronze Vishnu idol—wrapped in bubble wrap like a cheap vase. Mishra whispered:

"Sir, ee lagta hamaar Muzaffarpur waali hai… chhati dhadak rahal hai."

They planted a GPS and bug. Stepped out.

Twist: Betrayal

Just before the raid team moved, someone leaked the plan. Warehouse half empty.

"Idol truck mein daal ke nikal gaya, NH-48 route," a constable said.

Jai Deep jumped in his SUV.

"Mishra, saans rok ke baith jaa. Aaj murti bhi bachaayenge aur tera hawa mein udne ka sapna bhi pura hoga."

Highway Chase

Dawn fog. A silent SUV tailed a truck. At a dhaba turn, Jai Deep overtook and blocked its path.

"Truck khol. Abhi."

Driver hesitated. Jai Deep fired at the tire. Inside: the idol—intact.

Aftermath

Media went wild. But Jai Deep knew—this was just surface.

Sana decrypted another file:

Shipment list for next consignment: Kashmir Valley - Shiva idols - "Raabta - 3 months"

This isn’t over.

Closing Dialogue

"Sir, hum toh soch rahe the yeh chori ka case hai. Par yeh toh kahani jaada lambi lagta…"

Jai Deep stared at the map of India.

"Haan Mishra… aur is baar kahani mein sirf chor nahi hain… neta bhi hain."
