Red Siren Diaries – Chapter 1: “The Suicide That Wasn’t”

Set in Delhi | Genre: Crime Thriller | Tone: Sharp, Realistic, Inspirational

Amar Colony, South Delhi – July Morning

It was humid. Even the air felt like it was sweating.

ACP Jai Deep Rana, posted in Delhi Crime Branch, stood inside the minimalist yet modern flat of Rhea Malhotra, 27. The AC hummed, but it couldn’t erase the heaviness of the scene.

She lay on the ground — lifeless, cold, lips slightly parted. Empty bottle of sleeping pills in one hand, a half-written diary in the other.

“Sir… sab kuch suicide jaise hi lagta hai,” said Inspector Aditya Mishra, also known across the department as Mishra ji. “Darwaza andar se band tha. Koi tod-phod nahi. Maid ne subah 6:15 pe dekha, hum 7 baje aaye…”

Mishra, with his Bhojpuri swagger and loose shirt tucked only on one side, had a knack for being funny even at crime scenes. Originally from Buxar, he was more a sidekick from a 90s Bihari novel than a Delhi cop — yet sharp when it mattered.

But Jai Deep wasn’t convinced.

He slowly walked to the window, opened it. “There’s no dust on the sill… but no latch marks either.”

“Toh?” Mishra frowned.

“Toh yeh window kal raat ko saaf ki gayi hai. Kyu? Suicide karne se pehle koi window kyun saaf karega?”

He knelt next to Rhea’s hand. Something caught his eye — a nearly invisible fiber stuck under her ring.

A fine surveillance thread. “Yeh self-inflicted nahi hai. This is a murder. Covered up to look clean.”

Flashback – Jai Deep's Journey

As the forensics arrived, Jai Deep stepped outside on the balcony. He thought of his own battles — the ones no police report would ever mention.

His father, an Army Subedar, was furious when Jai Deep refused NDA. “Police waale kya karte hain? Gunde ban jaate hain license ke saath!”

But Jai Deep was built differently. Quiet, focused, and deep-thinking. He studied in Delhi during school years, did college in Gwalior, and cracked UPSC in his third attempt — while doing part-time jobs and living in a room so small he had to sleep diagonally.

Today, he wore the khaki uniform. Not to show it off. To clean what had rotted.

The Trail – Rhea’s Secret Life

Rhea was no ordinary woman. On paper, she worked for an NGO. Off the record, she was a data analyst for a shell company owned by Rishabh Jain, son of business tycoon Prakash Jain.

Prakash Jain had deep connections — including with MLA Bhupendra Rawat, a veteran South Delhi politician with a reputation for shutting mouths.

Rhea’s laptop was wiped clean. Her mobile had only basic calls.

Jai Deep visited Rhea’s father — a retired DU professor. In her childhood room, hidden inside an old Enid Blyton book, was a notebook filled with diagrams and handwritten entries:

  • Shell firms
  • Black money
  • Experimental drug trials
  • Fund transfers from government accounts to offshore ones

All roads led to Jain Pharma.

Action – The Arrest Operation

Jai Deep filed for a warrant against Rishabh Jain, but the file “got misplaced” in the system.

“MLA Rawat ne haath ghumaya hai,” Mishra said bitterly. “Par hum bhi Bihar ke hai, sir. Ego mein justice dhoond lete hain.”

Jai Deep got a tip: Rishabh was partying in a Chattarpur farmhouse. They raided it without warning. Amidst flashy lights and expensive vodka, Jai Deep found a hidden server room behind a bookshelf.

Inside — encrypted data, CCTV backups, and — the jackpot — a recording of Rishabh screaming at Rhea:

“Tu samajhti kya hai? Main MLA Rawat ka aadmi hoon! Agar kuch bola toh tujhe aur tere family ko khatam kar dunga!”

Courtroom Showdown

With evidence in hand, Jai Deep arrested Rishabh at his Golf Course Road penthouse. MLA Rawat tried to intimidate the court. But Jai Deep stood firm.

He submitted Rhea’s notebook, video files, and shell company data. “This isn’t just about one woman’s death,” he said in court. “It’s about an entire system hiding behind power.”

The judge denied bail. Rishabh was sent to Tihar Jail. Prakash Jain fled to Dubai. A Red Corner Notice was issued within hours.

Shadow Over the Hero

In a dark surveillance room, a man in a black shirt watched Jai Deep’s courtroom footage on loop. “ACP Jai Deep Rana…” he muttered, picking up a phone. “Start monitoring him. He’s not going to stop. Time to push back.”

Rhea’s Final Move

In Gwalior, a college professor named Dr. Isha Singh received a registered post. It was from Rhea. Inside — a pen drive and a note:

“If anything happens to me, this drive has names. Not just Jain. But bureaucrats. Police. Officers. This goes only to ACP Jai Deep Rana. No one else.”

One folder read: GREY LIST – Candidates / Corrupt / Embedded in System

The Next Case Begins…

Later that evening, Jai Deep sat in his quarters, sipping black tea. A brown envelope lay on his table.

“Mishra ji ne bheja hai,” said the constable.

He opened it. Inside — the pen drive from Rhea. Just then, his phone rang.

“Sir, Hauz Khas museum mein chori. Rare idol gayab. FIR ho gaya.”

Jai Deep stood up. Pocketed the drive. From the jeep, Mishra shouted, “Sir! Ee toh naya case lagta hai… lekin chori simple nahi lagti!”

Jai Deep smirked. “Let’s go. Red Siren Diaries ka agla page likhna hai.”
