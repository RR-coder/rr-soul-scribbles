Chapter 1: The Suicide That Wasn’t

Genre: Crime Thriller | Setting: Delhi | Tone: Sharp, Realistic, Inspirational

Scene 1: Amar Colony, South Delhi – July Morning

It was humid. Even the air felt like it was sweating.

ACP Jai Deep Rana, posted in Delhi Crime Branch, stood inside the minimalist yet modern flat of Rhea Malhotra, 27. The AC hummed, but it couldn’t erase the heaviness of the scene.

She lay on the ground — lifeless, cold, lips slightly parted. Empty bottle of sleeping pills in one hand, a half-written diary in the other.

“Sir… sab kuch suicide jaise hi lagta hai,” said Inspector Aditya Mishra, also known across the department as Mishra ji. “Darwaza andar se band tha. Koi tod-phod nahi. Maid ne subah 6:15 pe dekha, hum 7 baje aaye…”

Mishra, with his Bhojpuri swagger and a half-tucked shirt, always brought rough charm to chaos. Originally from Buxar, he was more sidekick from a 90s Bihari novel than a Delhi cop — but sharp where it counted.

But Jai Deep wasn’t convinced.

He walked slowly toward the window and opened it. “There’s no dust on the sill… but no latch marks either.”

“Toh?” Mishra frowned.

“Toh yeh window kal raat ko saaf ki gayi hai. Kyu? Suicide karne se pehle koi window kyun saaf karega?”

He knelt next to Rhea’s hand. Something glinted.

A fine surveillance fiber. Nearly invisible to the naked eye.

“Yeh self-inflicted nahi hai,” Jai Deep said. “This is a murder. Covered up to look clean.”

Flashback – Jai Deep’s Journey

As forensics arrived, Jai Deep stepped out to the balcony, sweat clinging to his back despite the early hour.

His thoughts went elsewhere. His father — an Army Subedar — had once said, “Police waale kya karte hain? Gunde ban jaate hain license ke saath!” The words had cut deep.

But Jai Deep didn’t want uniform for power. He wanted it for purification. He had lived in rented rooms the size of a godown shelf. Had studied under fans that worked only in prayer. His third UPSC attempt had broken him — but also made him.

And today, he was ACP Jai Deep Rana — not because the system allowed him to be. But because he refused to let it swallow him.

Scene 2: The Trail – Rhea’s Secret Life

On the surface, Rhea was just another NGO employee.

But Jai Deep found inconsistencies in her transactions. Her bank logs showed regular, small transfers from a “consulting firm” in Gurgaon — but the address led to a closed shop.

When her mobile yielded nothing, Jai Deep visited her father — a retired DU professor. The room was still like a temple to memory. Her bookshelf held dusty Enid Blytons.

Inside one, hidden behind brittle pages, was a slim blue notebook.

The entries made Jai Deep’s pulse quicken:

  • Shell firms
  • Black money
  • Experimental drug trials
  • Fund transfers from government accounts

All roads led to Jain Pharma.

“Murder to pehla step tha sir,” Mishra murmured. “Yeh toh jungle hai.”

Scene 3: Action – The Arrest Operation

Jai Deep filed for a warrant to arrest Rishabh Jain, son of business magnate Prakash Jain.

But two days passed.

Then three.

The file never reached the magistrate.

“System mein kahin bhi nahi mil raha,” said the clerk.

“Mil raha hoga Rawat ke table pe,” Mishra added. “MLA Rawat ne haath ghumaya hai… Par hum bhi Bihar ke hain, sir. Ego mein justice dhoond lete hain.”

A tip-off came — Rishabh was hosting a rave at a Chattarpur farmhouse. Without waiting for fresh paperwork, Jai Deep mobilized his team.

🔥 Raid Night – Chattarpur

High boundary walls. Z-plus security. The muffled thump of bass-heavy EDM leaked into the humid Chattarpur air.

ACP Jai Deep Rana crouched behind a neem tree near the rear wall, eyes scanning the perimeter.

“Sir,” whispered Mishra ji, panting slightly, “ee log toh rave karat hain... lekin andar kuch aur hi pak raha hai.”

Jai Deep’s jaw tightened. “Rave toh cover hai. We go in low and silent.”

They hoisted themselves up the compound wall with a grappling hook, constables Ravi and Deepender close behind. As soon as their boots hit the manicured lawn, a flashlight swept past.

“Move!” Jai Deep gestured, and they pressed themselves behind a tall hedge.

A guard passed by — smoking, bored. Mishra muttered, “Ee security waala zyada dekh le, toh hum hospital se inquiry karein ka?”

Jai Deep gave a tight smirk. “Focus, Mishra ji.”

Near the side entrance, a service gate clicked open. A young man walked out to vomit near the bushes.

Within three seconds, Ravi had clamped a hand over his mouth while Mishra whispered, “Chillaile toh party ke andar hi thanda kar denge... samjha?”

The man nodded frantically.

Inside, laser lights sliced through the thick air. Champagne flowed like water. DJ thumped over flashing speakers. Half-naked influencers danced. Politician’s kids laughed in private booths.

“Rave nahi hai, sir,” Mishra leaned in, scanning a group of suited men at the bar, “Yeh toh system ke beta party hai.”

Jai Deep’s gaze swept the walls. “They’re hiding something.”

They moved past the lounge area toward a hallway labeled “Private Access – Staff Only.”

Two guards stood at the door.

“Sir, direct takkar lete hain?” Mishra whispered.

“Too loud. Make it clean.”

Mishra pulled out a bottle of “foreign scotch” from a confiscated stash and swaggered up to the guards.

“Bhai log! MLA sahab ne bhejwaaya… ek peg toh banta hai!”

As they looked confused, Deepender hit one with a nerve punch. Jai Deep caught the other’s rifle mid-air before he could raise it.

Door unlocked.

Beyond it, silence. Dim lights. Sterile walls. Server hum.

They turned a corridor and found a mahogany bookshelf with mismatched edges.

“Books in a rave?” Mishra scoffed.

Jai Deep ran his finger along the spines. One stuck. He pulled it. Click. The shelf slid open — revealing a glass chamber behind it.

Inside — a temperature-controlled server room. Red indicator lights blinked like eyes.

“This isn’t just storage. It’s a brain,” Jai Deep muttered.

Mishra stepped back in awe. “Sir… ee toh RAW waale log bhi nahi dekh paate aise data room!”

Dozens of hard drives, encrypted routers, and high-speed servers filled the chamber. Screens showed recent activity. One was playing back a security feed. Jai Deep clicked through.

Then — a recording popped up. Blurry video. Voices shouting. Rishabh Jain — his face flushed with rage — stared directly into the camera, yelling:

“Tu samajhti kya hai? Main MLA Rawat ka aadmi hoon! Agar kuch bola toh tujhe aur tere family ko khatam kar dunga!”

“Ee… Rhea se baat kar raha hai, na?” Mishra whispered.

Jai Deep nodded, silent, seething.

He clicked “Save” and backed up the footage into a portable SSD from his coat.

A loud thud echoed outside. One of the guards was awake.

“Sir... time ho gaya bhaag chalne ka,” Mishra said quickly, “warna hum sab ka data bhi yahin upload ho jayega permanent.”

As they turned to exit, one last screen blinked: 🟥 “Grey List – Internal Leaks / Media Contacts / Embedded Assets”

Jai Deep took a photo. “We're coming back for this.”

Scene 4: Courtroom Showdown

Tis Hazari Court – 3 Days Later

Jai Deep stood across from Rishabh, now dressed in white kurta, trying to look like an obedient son.

The courtroom was tense. Media outside. Political aides inside.

Judge Sharma looked up after scanning the file. “You’re saying the death was made to look like suicide?”

“Yes, My Lord,” Jai Deep said, handing over the ring fiber analysis, the notebook, and digital recordings. “We have motive, method, and voice.”

Rishabh’s lawyer objected. “This is fabricated! There is no forensic stamp—”

Jai Deep snapped back: “Because the lab was told to delay it! Orders came from MLA Rawat’s office, which is also involved.”

The judge leaned forward. “Bail is denied. Accused to be remanded to Tihar Jail. Forensic verification to be fast-tracked. MLA Rawat’s office will be investigated independently.”

Outside, Mishra exhaled. “Game badal gaya, sir.”

Jai Deep replied, “Game toh ab shuru hua hai.”

Scene 5: Shadow Over the Hero

Inside a black surveillance room, a man in a high-backed chair rewound courtroom footage.

He wore a black shirt, hands resting on a polished table.

“ACP Jai Deep Rana…” he muttered.

He picked up the phone. “Start monitoring him. He’s not going to stop. Time to push back.”

Scene 6: Rhea’s Final Move

Gwalior – A Quiet Campus Office

Professor Dr. Isha Singh received a registered envelope from Delhi.

Inside — a pen drive and a handwritten note.

“If anything happens to me, this drive has names. Not just Jain. But bureaucrats. Police. Officers. This goes only to ACP Jai Deep Rana. No one else.”

The drive had one folder: 🟥 GREY LIST – Candidates / Corrupt / Embedded in System

Final Scene: The Next Case Begins

Back in Delhi, Jai Deep sat with his black tea. A constable walked in.

“Sir, ye envelope Mishra ji ne bheja hai.”

Inside — the pen drive.

Just then, his phone rang.

“Sir, Hauz Khas museum mein chori. Rare idol gayab. FIR ho gaya.”

Jai Deep stood. Pocketed the drive.

From the jeep, Mishra ji shouted, “Sir! Ee toh naya case lagta hai… lekin chori simple nahi lagti!”

Jai Deep smirked. “Let’s go. Red Siren Diaries ka agla page likhna hai.”
